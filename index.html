Selected Research

CARE

Erasing Without Collateral Damage: Precise Concept Removal in Diffusion Models

Preprint · under review, 2026

A training-free concept-erasure framework (CARE) for diffusion models. Rather than removing a concept along a raw direction, which harms semantically related concepts, CARE erases along a retained-subspace-aware direction computed offline from kept-concept anchors, adding only ~0.7% per-image overhead while preserving what should stay. Built on the AdaVD codebase (Wang et al., CVPR 2025).

LDAT

Learning Dynamics-Aware Training for Long-Tailed Medical Image Classification

Under review, 2026

LDAT is a closed-loop training framework that sets per-class loss weights from observed learning dynamics (velocity, acceleration, relative progress) instead of static class frequencies. It reaches 84.0% accuracy on ISIC2018 in 50 epochs — matching or beating methods that require 200–1,000 epochs — at negligible overhead.

QC-SMOTE: Quality-Controlled Oversampling for Imbalanced Classification

In preparation

A quality-controlled variant of SMOTE that scores each minority seed for trustworthiness, allocates synthesis effort by reliability, and selects the best of K candidates via an interpolation-purity metric. Across 30 KEEL benchmarks (imbalance ratio up to 32.7) it reaches 0.931 AUC-ROC, improving over VS-SMOTE.
